# Air Raid 🎯 (CFB26 Playbook Style)

[← Back to Index](../README.md)

## Quick definition 🧠

The Air Raid is a **spread passing system** built on:

- 🔄 High repetition
- 📐 Spacing and leverage
- 👓 Simple reads that scale from quick game to deep shots
- 🏃 Using short passes as “runs” to stay on schedule

```text
      WR          LT LG C RG RT          WR
           SB                        SB
                    QB (Gun)
                       RB
```

It’s at its best when you can reliably throw on early downs, force the defense to widen, and then **run into light boxes**.

## The identity (one sentence) 🎯

**Throw efficient, high-percentage routes until the defense breaks, then hit the seam or the deep shot.**

---

## Pros ✅ and cons ⚠️

### Pros

- **Elite down-to-down consistency** when your QB is accurate and you can identify coverage.
- **Answers vs pressure**: built-in hot routes, quick game, and spacing concepts punish blitzes.
- **Easy to call** once your “base” menu is set (you don’t need 30 formations).
- **Maximizes WR skill**: separation, route running, and RAC matter more than raw size.
- **Tempo-friendly**: you can play fast without complex checks.

### Cons

- Can become **too horizontal** if you don’t take vertical shots on schedule.
- **Red zone tightening**: spacing compresses inside the 15; you need goal-line answers.
- If your QB is inaccurate under heat, the system can **stall quickly** (incompletions stop clock).
- Some defenses will sit in **two-high shells** and rally/tackle, forcing you to string long drives.

## Typical personnel (who’s on the field) 🧩

Air Raid playbooks commonly live in:

- **10 personnel**: 1 RB, 0 TE, 4 WR
  - Great for spacing, screens, shallow cross, and empty.
  - Risk: pass pro can be fragile; you must punish light boxes.
- **11 personnel**: 1 RB, 1 TE, 3 WR
  - Most balanced Air Raid base.
  - TE can be a seam/bender target and a protection helper.
- **20 personnel**: 2 RB, 0 TE, 3 WR
  - Used for “pony” packages (two backs) to force mismatches.

### Personnel cheat table

| Personnel | On-field           | Why you’d use it in Air Raid                          |
| --------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 10        | 1 RB / 4 WR        | Maximum spacing; quick game and empty variations      |
| 11        | 1 RB / 1 TE / 3 WR | Balanced base; seams/benders + better protection help |
| 20        | 2 RB / 3 WR        | Pony mismatches; easy checkdowns and pass-pro options |

### Role archetypes (more useful than position labels)

- **QB:** accurate short-to-intermediate, quick decision-maker, calm vs pressure.
- **RB:** good hands + pass pro + ability to win on draw/inside zone.
- **WR1 (X):** can win isolation (slants, fades, glance, comebacks).
- **Slot(s):** twitch + option route IQ + reliable hands.
- **TE (if used):** seam threat or physical blocker (depends on your flavor).

## Typical formations (what you should major in) 📐

You don’t need many. Pick a small “family” and get great at it.

**Core Air Raid families (common across EA-style playbooks):**

- **Gun Trips / Trips Open**: spacing, stick, verts, flood, screens.
- **Gun Doubles (2x2)**: mesh, Y-cross, four verts, shallow.
- **Gun Empty**: quick game, spacing, option routes, quick outs.
- **Gun Bunch**: natural picks/rubs, flood, corner routes, quick screens.

### Formation families (quick pick table)

| Family            | What it’s great for                                  | When to lean on it             |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Gun Trips         | Spacing/stick/verts; consistent reads                | Early downs; tempo drives      |
| Gun Doubles (2x2) | Mesh/Y-Cross/verts; balanced access                  | Base menu; 3rd-and-medium      |
| Gun Empty         | Quick game; option routes; fast “get it out” answers | 2-minute; vs heavy blitz looks |
| Gun Bunch         | Rubs/picks; flood; red zone spacing                  | Red zone; short yardage passes |

**A simple “major/minor” menu:**

- Major: Trips + Doubles
- Minor: Empty (third-and-medium), Bunch (red zone / short yardage passes)

## Core concept menu (the Air Raid “greatest hits”)

The trick is not knowing 50 plays; it’s mastering **a small set of concepts** and how they beat different coverages.

### 1) Stick / Spacing (quick game)

**What it is:** quick outs + hooks/option route to create a fast, safe completion.

**When to call:**

- 1st & 10, 2nd & 6, any time you want an easy completion
- vs blitz looks or soft zone

**What you’re reading:**

- Apex/flat defender: if he widens, hit the hitch; if he sits, throw the out.

**Common counters:**

- If defense squats the flat: pump-and-go or switch release.

### 2) Mesh (man-beater + zone settle)

**What it is:** two shallow crossers with a sit route and a corner/post over the top.

**Why it works:** creates traffic vs man and spacing vs zone.

**Coaching points:**

- Don’t force the crossers immediately; confirm leverage.
- Against zone, look for the sit/hook window behind the shallow.

**Add-on:** motion to identify man/zone.

### 3) Four Verticals (with seam reads)

**What it is:** vertical stems that attack the safeties.

**When to call:**

- vs single-high after you’ve forced them to play honest underneath
- 2nd & short, after a big completion, or after a run that forces safety rotation

**QB coaching points (simple version):**

- Identify shell (1-high vs 2-high).
- Against 2-high: seams can split; take the best matchup and lead away from safeties.
- Against 1-high: outside verts can win; seam can occupy the middle safety.

### 4) Y-Cross / Shallow Cross (intermediate dagger)

**What it is:** a deep crossing route paired with a shallow and a backside dig/post.

**Why it works:** it layers defenders and punishes zone rotations.

**How to use it in-game:**

- If users or AI overplays the deep cross, hit the shallow early for RAC.
- If linebackers widen to the shallow, throw the cross behind them.

### 5) Screens (WR + RB)

Screens are a major Air Raid “run game.”

**WR screens:**

- Great vs off-coverage, cushion, and aggressive pass rush.
- Pair with a backside slant or glance if they start triggering.

**RB screens:**

- Great vs blitz and man coverage.
- Coach point: don’t drift too deep before throwing; timing matters.

### 6) Draw / Inside Zone (the honest run)

You still need a real run call to punish light boxes.

- **Inside zone**: staple; read the box count.
- **Draw**: great in empty or pass-heavy tendencies.

## Building your Air Raid call sheet (practical CFB26 plan)

This section is intentionally actionable: you can copy it into your in-game favorites.

### 1st and 10 (stay efficient)

- Stick / spacing
- Inside zone (or split zone)
- Quick slants (vs soft press)
- Bubble/now screen (if corners are off)

**Rule of thumb:** take the free 5–7 yards repeatedly until safeties start cheating.

### 2nd and short (take a shot)

- Four verts
- Post + dig / “dagger” concepts
- Play-action shot (if your playbook includes it from gun)

### 2nd and long (don’t panic)

- Mesh
- Shallow cross
- RB screen

### 3rd down

- 3rd & 1–2: quick out, stick, inside zone, or bunch quick rub
- 3rd & 3–6: mesh, spacing with an option route, shallow
- 3rd & 7+: Y-cross, verts (with protection), RB checkdown built-in

### Red zone (inside the 20)

Spacing shrinks; win with **leverage** and **pick concepts**.

- Bunch flood / corner routes
- Smash (corner + hitch)
- Slant/flat
- Fade + underneath option

### Goal line (inside the 5)

- Pick/rub concepts from bunch or stack
- Quick sprint-out if your QB throws well on the move
- Power run if they go dime

### Backed up (own 1–10)

- Inside zone
- Stick (fast throw)
- RB screen (safe vs pressure)

### Two-minute (favorites)

- Sideline outs, quick seams, and stop routes
- Use screens as “clock-friendly runs” if you can get out of bounds

## Audibles and pre-snap plan

You want an audible set that answers:

- a heavy box
- a light box
- a blitz
- a soft zone shell

**Suggested audible package (example):**

1. Inside Zone (run)
2. Stick / Spacing (quick)
3. Mesh (man/pressure answer)
4. Four Verticals (shot)
5. RB Screen (blitz punish)

### The “box-count” habit

- If the defense shows **5 or fewer** in the box vs your spread set: consider running.
- If the defense shows **6+** and corners are off: take quick game.
- If you see obvious pressure: keep a screen or quick slant ready.

## How defenses try to stop the Air Raid (and what to do)

### Two-high, rally-and-tackle

**What you’ll see:** Cover 2 / Cover 4 shells, soft underneath.

**Your answers:**

- Keep taking quick game and be patient.
- Run inside zone/draw to punish light boxes.
- Call seams/benders or switch verts when they get too passive.

### Press man + pressure

**What you’ll see:** tight corners, blitzes, forced quick throws.

**Your answers:**

- Mesh and rubs from bunch/stack.
- RB screens.
- Slant/flat and quick fades (only if your WR wins consistently).

### User lurk / linebacker baiting

**What you’ll see:** user defender sitting in the hook/crosser lane.

**Your answers:**

- Throw away from the user: outs, flats, or backside hitches.
- Use motion to change who the user is responsible for.
- Call a concept that forces the user to choose (high-low him).

## Roster building (what to prioritize)

If you’re building a Dynasty roster, Air Raid priorities are usually:

1. **QB accuracy + awareness** (quick reads matter more than arm strength)
2. **WR separation** (route running / release / catching)
3. **Pass protection** (especially tackles)
4. **RB hands + pass pro**
5. **One seam threat** (TE or big slot)

Speed is nice, but consistent separation is what keeps drives alive.

## Coverage cheat sheet (how your core concepts “want” to win)

Air Raid is easiest when you understand what each concept is trying to stress.

### Stick / spacing

- Loves: soft zone, off coverage, blitz looks
- Struggles: squat corners and hard-flat defenders jumping outs

Counter ideas:

- pump-and-go
- switch releases

### Mesh

- Loves: man coverage (traffic), pressure
- Also good: zone (sit route settling behind shallows)

### Four verts / seams

- Loves: single-high when you can isolate outside
- Also good: two-high if you can split seams and throw on time

### Y-cross / shallow

- Loves: zone rotations and match coverage
- The shallow is the “get out of jail,” the cross is the chunk

### Screens

- Loves: aggressive pass rush and off coverage
- Struggles: corners that trigger instantly and tackle well

Counter idea:

- screen-and-go / sluggo / tunnel screen (depending on your playbook)

## Protection and “sack avoidance” rules

Air Raid is about staying on schedule. A sack is often worse than a throwaway.

Practical rules:

- If your first read isn’t there by the time the rush arrives, take the checkdown or throw it away.
- Against heavy blitz, lean on:
  - quick game
  - screens
  - empty only if you have immediate throws

If you notice a specific edge rusher wrecking you, avoid calling longer-developing concepts toward him.

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: “I complete passes but never score.”

Fix:

- call a shot on 2nd & short (don’t wait until 3rd & 12)
- use seams and switch verts once the defense widens

### Problem: “I throw picks on outs.”

Fix:

- stop throwing late to the flat
- throw hitch/inside option instead when the flat defender widens

### Problem: “The defense just sits in two-high.”

Fix:

- run inside zone/draw more often
- keep taking 5–7 yard completions and stay patient

## Minimal glossary

- **Apex defender:** the player between slot and box; key for RPO/spacing reads.
- **Leverage:** defender positioning; throw away from it.
- **RAC:** run after catch.
- **Shell:** the pre-snap safety structure (1-high / 2-high).
