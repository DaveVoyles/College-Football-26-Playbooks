# Veer and Shoot 🚀 (CFB26 Playbook Style)

Author: Dave Voyles

[← Back to Index](../README.md)

## Quick definition 🧠

Veer and Shoot is a **wide-split, tempo-friendly spread offense** that tries to:

- ↔️ force defenses to declare numbers and leverage with **extreme spacing**
- 🏃 run “veer” (inside zone / split-flow looks) into light boxes
- 🎯 throw **quick access passes** (now screens, hitches, quick outs) when defenders widen
- 💣 hit vertical shots when safeties cheat

```text
WR (Far out)                                   WR (Far out)
           SB        OL        SB
        (Slot)                (Slot)
                     QB
                     RB
```

In real football it’s often associated with **ultra-wide WR splits**, fast pace, and simple, repeatable reads that create “easy yards” over and over.

## The identity (one sentence) 🎯

**Spread the defense to the sideline, take the free yards immediately, and gas them with tempo until a vertical shot breaks the game open.**

---

## Pros ✅ and cons ⚠️

```diff
+ PRO: Clear pre-snap answers (spacing = information).
+ PRO: Tempo leverage (prevents subs, creates chaos).
+ PRO: Explosive potential (one missed tackle = TD).
+ PRO: Easy to teach/call (small menu).
+ PRO: Hides average OL (ball comes out fast).

- CON: Can feel too horizontal (need to shot vertically).
- CON: Negative plays kill drives (must stay on schedule).
- CON: Needs WR blocking (perimeter game is run game).
- CON: Quick decision fatigue (late throws = INT).
- CON: Defenses can rally and tackle in space.
```

## Typical personnel (who’s on the field) 🧩

Most Veer and Shoot looks are built from:

- **10 personnel** (1 RB, 0 TE, 4 WR): maximum spacing
- **11 personnel** (1 RB, 1 TE, 3 WR): adds a seam/flat element and protection help

### Personnel cheat table

| Personnel | On-field           | Why you’d use it in Veer and Shoot                    |
| --------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 10        | 1 RB / 4 WR        | Widest spacing, best for screens + access throws      |
| 11        | 1 RB / 1 TE / 3 WR | Better protection; TE seams and RPO tags              |
| 20        | 2 RB / 3 WR        | “Pony” mismatch package; good for option/RPO variants |

### Role archetypes (what matters) 🔎

- **QB:** quick processor, accurate short, calm vs pressure; arm strength helps for vertical shots.
- **RB:** one-cut runner + acceleration; must be comfortable catching and pass protecting.
- **WRs:** hands + RAC + willingness to block; at least one true vertical threat.
- **Slot(s):** option/choice capable (if your playbook has them) and reliable on quick game.
- **TE (if used):** seam threat and edge helper.

## Splits, alignment, and why it changes everything 🧷

One of the defining “feel” features of Veer and Shoot is **how wide the offense aligns**.

### What wide splits do for you

- They **remove bodies from the box** (defenders have to widen to respect access throws).
- They create cleaner, easier-to-see **run lanes** for inside zone.
- They make coverage rules harder for defenses (match coverage communication gets stressed).

### The trade-off

- Wide splits can make some route breaks feel longer.
- If you hold the ball waiting for a “pretty” window, pressure arrives.

**Coaching point:** wide splits are a promise to play fast. If you want to hold the ball and progress 1–2–3 slowly, you’re drifting into a different style.

## Core concept menu (the engine room) ⚙️

The Veer and Shoot is about repeating a small set of concepts until the defense overreacts.

### 1) Inside zone / split flow (“veer” feel)

**Goal:** punish light boxes created by wide splits.

**When to call:**

- vs two-high shells
- anytime the box count is light

**Coaching point:** be patient and cut backside if the front side is muddy.

### 2) Access throws (free yards) 🏁

These are the “take it if they give it” passes:

- hitch/now
- quick out
- quick smoke

**Read:** corner cushion + alignment.

```diff
+ GREEN:  Corner Off (6+ yds) -> THROW the Hitch/Now
- RED:    Corner Press/Cloud  -> HAND OFF inside
```

- If the corner is off and bailing: take the hitch/now.
- If the corner squats hard: you need a counter (double-move or run).

### 3) Screens (perimeter run game) 🫧

- bubble/now screen
- tunnel screen

**Why they matter:** they force overhang defenders to widen, which makes inside zone easier.

### 4) RPO tags (if present) 🧠

Common tags:

- bubble
- glance/slant
- stick/spot

**Conflict defender:** often the apex/overhang.

If he fits run: throw behind him.
If he widens: hand it off.

### 5) Vertical shots (the “shoot”) 💥

You need a small shot menu so defenses can’t just rally to the flat all day.

Common vertical families:

- seams / four verts variants
- post
- switch vertical

**When to call (on schedule):**

- 2nd & short
- after two consecutive access completions
- after a long run when safeties start creeping

| What you see           | What it’s trying to do       | Your best answers                                    |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Two-high, light box    | Invite run, rally to throws  | Inside zone; screens; patient access throws          |

| Press man              | Disrupt timing               | Stack/bunch if available; tunnel screens; crossers   |
| Heavy blitz            | Speed up QB                  | Screens, quick game, RB swing/flat, max protect shot |

## Building your Veer and Shoot call sheet (practical CFB26 plan) 🗂️

### 1st and 10 (stay efficient)

- inside zone
- hitch/now (if cushion)
- bubble/now screen
- RPO bubble/glance (if in your book)

### 2nd and short (take a shot) 💥

- seams / four verts
- post
- switch vertical

### 2nd and long (get back on schedule)

- screen
- quick out/hitch
- inside zone if the box is still light

### 3rd down

- 3rd & 1–2: inside zone, quick hitch, QB keep (if available)
- 3rd & 3–6: access throw + concept that stretches flats (stick/spacing)
- 3rd & 7+: seams/verts with a checkdown, or a crosser concept if included

### Red zone (inside 20) 🧱

The field compresses. Shift from “space” to “leverage.”

- smash (corner/hitch)
- bunch/stack rubs (if available)
- quick slants with leverage
- inside zone if the defense spreads out too far

### Two-minute ⏱️

- quick outs to the boundary
- seams when safeties widen
- screens if you can get out of bounds

## Tempo plan (use pace without self-destructing) ⚡

Tempo is a weapon, but only if you protect yourself from chaos.

### When to go fast

- after a first down on an access throw
- after a successful inside zone
- when the defense is stuck in a bad personnel group

### When to slow down

- after a penalty
- after a tackle for loss
- after a near-interception

**Rule of thumb:** tempo until a negative play happens, then reset.

---

## The “two-play loop” (how Veer and Shoot stays efficient) 🔁

When you’re not sure what to call, Veer and Shoot should fall back to a simple loop.

### Loop A: run-first

1. Inside zone
2. Access throw (hitch/now) if cushion, otherwise inside zone again

### Loop B: screen-first

1. Bubble/now screen
2. Inside zone (because overhang defenders widened)

The point isn’t that these are your only plays—it’s that your base identity always has a stable answer.

## Troubleshooting 🧯

### Problem: “My access throws get jumped.”

Fix:

- stop throwing late; access throws must be instant
- mix in inside zone more often
- add a tunnel screen or a shot concept after two quick completions

### Problem: “I can’t run inside zone.”

Fix:

- call more screens to widen overhang defenders
- check your box count (don’t run into heavy boxes)
- use a constraint run (counter/split zone) if available

### Problem: “Tempo makes me go 3-and-out.”

Fix:

- tempo only on your best two concepts (inside zone + access)
- slow down after negative plays

## Practice-mode routine (10 minutes) 🏋️

If you want the scheme to feel automatic:

1. Run inside zone 10 reps and focus on cutback lanes.
2. Throw 10 access throws (hitch/now) and focus on releasing the ball instantly.
3. Call 5 screens and focus on WR blocking angles.
4. Call your favorite seams/verts shot 5 times to learn the timing.

If you do this once per “week” in Dynasty, the offense will start to feel like muscle memory.

## Shot-call triggers (when to take the “shoot” throw) 🎆

Veer and Shoot becomes unstoppable when you stop forcing shots and start taking them **only when the defense tells you it’s time**.

Great triggers:

- Safeties creeping down after multiple access completions
- Corners squatting on hitches/now
- Overhang defenders widening hard to screens (seams open behind them)

If you don’t see a trigger, keep stacking efficient plays. The explosive usually arrives because the defense gets impatient.

## Minimal glossary 📌

- **Access throw:** immediate throw to a WR with cushion (hitch/now/out).
- **Veer (modern usage):** inside zone / split-flow family that punishes light boxes.
- **Shoot:** vertical emphasis (seams/posts) that punishes safeties cheating.
- **Wide splits:** WR alignments near the sideline to stretch coverage.
